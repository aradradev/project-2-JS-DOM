What is the imposter syndrome?

Imposter syndrome is a psychological pattern where an individual doubts their own accomplishments and feels like a fraud despite evidence of success. It can lead to feelings of inadequacy, anxiety, and self-doubt, and is often experienced by high-achievers who fear being exposed as incompetent. The condition can be overcome through therapy, positive self-talk, and recognition of one's own accomplishments. It is important to recognize and address imposter syndrome in oneself and others, as it can have a significant impact on mental health and career success.
